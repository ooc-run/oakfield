# Oakfield Code Of Conduct

Oakfield is a technical project, but the quality of the work
depends on the quality of the room. Be clear, patient, and direct. Assume good
intent while still taking impact seriously.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful of different backgrounds, experience levels, and communication
  styles.
- Keep feedback specific to the work: code, tests, documentation, benchmarks,
  reproducibility, and user impact.
- Welcome questions from new contributors and point them toward current docs or
  tasks when possible.
- Disclose uncertainty, especially for numerical accuracy, backend support, and
  experimental APIs.
- Help maintain a harassment-free space in issues, reviews, discussions, and
  project channels.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Sexualized language or attention.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussion after maintainers redirect it.
- Dismissing reproducible safety, accuracy, or accessibility concerns without
  engaging with the evidence.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns to the maintainers through the repository's private
contact path when one is available, or by opening a minimal issue that asks a
maintainer to establish a private channel. Maintainers should respond promptly,
preserve confidentiality where possible, and take proportionate action.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or delete comments, request behavior changes, pause
participation, or remove contributors from project spaces. The goal is a
productive, humane project where people can do careful work.
